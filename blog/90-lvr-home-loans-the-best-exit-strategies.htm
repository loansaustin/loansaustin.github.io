90 LVR Home Loans: The Best Exit Strategies

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The dream of homeownership often begins with a 90% Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR) loan. It’s the key that unlocks the door for countless individuals and families, allowing them to get a foothold on the property ladder without a colossal deposit. However, this opportunity comes with a significant caveat: you are starting your financial journey with minimal equity, making you highly susceptible to the economic tremors that define our current era. A 90 LVR loan isn't a permanent state; it's a strategic starting point that demands a clear and proactive exit plan. In a world grappling with persistent inflation, aggressive interest rate hikes, and geopolitical instability, having a robust strategy to escape a high LVR loan is not just prudent—it's essential for your long-term financial resilience.

The Perilous Position of 90 LVR in Today's Economic Climate

To understand why an exit strategy is non-negotiable, one must first appreciate the unique vulnerabilities of a high LVR mortgage holder in the current macroeconomic landscape.

The Double-Edged Sword of Leverage

A 90 LVR loan is the epitome of leverage. You are controlling a large asset with a relatively small amount of your own capital. While this magnifies your potential gains if property prices rise, it dramatically amplifies your risks when the tide turns. With only 10% equity, even a modest market correction of 5-10% can plunge you into negative equity, meaning you owe the bank more than your property is worth. This situation severely limits your options, making it difficult to refinance and trapping you with your current lender, potentially at less favorable rates.

Interest Rate Shock and the Cost-of-Living Squeeze

The last few years have been a brutal awakening for borrowers accustomed to historically low interest rates. Central banks, in a fierce battle against multi-decade high inflation, have embarked on the most aggressive monetary tightening cycle in a generation. For someone with a 90 LVR loan, which often already comes with higher interest rates due to the perceived risk (often called Lenders Mortgage Insurance or LMI), these rapid hikes translate directly into hundreds, if not thousands, of dollars in additional monthly repayments. This "mortgage shock" collides with a parallel crisis: the soaring cost of living. From groceries and energy to fuel and healthcare, household budgets are being stretched to their breaking point. For a 90 LVR borrower, this combination is a perfect storm, threatening mortgage stress and financial instability.

Geopolitical Unrest and Market Volatility

The stability of your local housing market is no longer just a function of local supply and demand. It is intricately linked to global events. Ongoing conflicts, supply chain disruptions, and international sanctions create ripple effects that impact economic growth, consumer confidence, and, consequently, property values. In such an environment, assuming steady, predictable capital growth to naturally lower your LVR is a risky gamble. A proactive exit strategy insulates you from these unpredictable global headwinds.

The Ultimate Goal: Reaching the 80 LVR Sanctuary

The primary objective for any 90 LVR borrower should be to breach the 80 LVR threshold as swiftly as possible. This is a critical milestone in the homeownership journey. Why is 80 LVR so magical?

  • Elimination of Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI): For most borrowers, once the LVR drops below 80%, the costly LMI premium is no longer required. This is a massive financial win, effectively reducing your overall debt.
  • Access to Better Interest Rates: Lenders view borrowers with an LVR of 80% or lower as significantly less risky. This opens the door to a much wider and more competitive range of home loan products with substantially lower interest rates, saving you a fortune over the life of the loan.
  • Financial Flexibility and Security: Reaching 20% equity provides a crucial buffer against market downturns, making negative equity far less likely. It also unlocks other financial opportunities, such as the ability to access equity for renovations or other investments.

With this goal in mind, let's explore the most effective exit strategies to get you from 90 to 80 LVR and beyond.

Proactive Exit Strategy #1: The Power of Aggressive Repayments

The most straightforward path to building equity is to pay down your principal loan balance faster than the scheduled amortization.

Embracing the Fortnightly Payment Cycle

One of the simplest yet most powerful tactics is to switch from monthly to fortnightly repayments. There are 26 fortnights in a year, which is equivalent to 13 monthly payments, not 12. By making half your monthly payment every two weeks, you inadvertently make one full extra monthly payment each year. This extra payment goes directly toward paying down your principal, significantly accelerating your equity growth and shaving years off your loan term.

Voluntary Lump-Sum Payments

Whenever a windfall comes your way—a tax refund, a work bonus, an inheritance, or even consistent savings from a side hustle—direct a portion of it as a lump-sum payment against your mortgage principal. Most loan accounts allow for substantial extra repayments without penalty. Even modest, regular lump sums can have a dramatic compound effect over time.

Requesting a "Recast" of Your Mortgage

After making a sizable lump-sum payment, inquire with your lender about "recasting" or "re-amortizing" your loan. This process recalculates your monthly payment based on the new, lower principal balance, while keeping the same loan term. The result is a lower required monthly payment, reducing your immediate financial pressure while you continue to own the property. This is different from refinancing, as it typically involves a small administrative fee rather than a full loan application process.

Proactive Exit Strategy #2: The Strategic Refinance

Refinancing is a potent tool, but it requires careful timing and consideration, especially for a 90 LVR borrower.

Timing the Market and Your Equity

Refinancing with an LVR still above 80% is often challenging and may not yield the best rates, as you'll still be subject to LMI. The most opportune moment to refinance is typically after you have convincingly crossed the 80 LVR threshold through repayments or capital growth. At this point, you can shop around for a lender offering a significantly lower interest rate. The savings on interest can then be redirected into even higher repayments, creating a virtuous cycle of debt reduction.

The "Cash-Out" Refinance for Value-Add

This is an advanced strategy that carries risk but can be highly effective. If your property's value has increased significantly, you might find that even without aggressive repayments, your LVR has fallen below 80%. In this scenario, you could potentially do a "cash-out" refinance. For example, if your home is now worth $800,000 and your loan is $600,000 (75 LVR), you could refinance to a new $640,000 loan (80 LVR of $800k), pulling out $40,000 in tax-free cash. This money can then be used for a high-impact renovation—like adding a bathroom or modernizing a kitchen—that further increases the property's value, thereby pushing your LVR even lower and building more equity. This must be done with a clear, value-adding plan, not for frivolous spending.

Proactive Exit Strategy #3: Forced Appreciation Through Property Enhancement

You are not solely at the mercy of the market; you can actively manufacture equity through strategic improvements to your property.

High-ROI Renovations

Focus on projects that offer the best return on investment (ROI). Cosmetic upgrades like a fresh coat of paint, new landscaping, and modernizing light fixtures and door handles are low-cost but can enhance curb appeal and perceived value. More substantial projects like kitchen and bathroom updates typically offer strong returns. The goal is to increase the appraised value of your home through verifiable improvements, which in turn lowers your LVR.

The "Granny Flat" or Auxiliary Dwelling Unit (ADU)

In many regions, adding a legal rental unit to your property—a granny flat, a converted basement, or a standalone ADU—can be a game-changer. Not only does this addition significantly boost the property's market value (and thus your equity), but it also creates a new income stream. The rental income from the ADU can be used to aggressively pay down the mortgage on the primary residence, turbocharging your journey to an 80 LVR.

The Safety Net Strategy: Preparing for the Unexpected

A truly robust exit plan also includes contingencies for worst-case scenarios.

Building an Emergency "Mortgage Buffer" Fund

Before channeling every spare dollar into your mortgage, prioritize building a cash emergency fund that covers 3-6 months of essential living expenses, including your mortgage repayments. This fund acts as a shock absorber for life's unexpected events—job loss, medical issues, major repairs—preventing you from falling behind on payments or, in a worst-case scenario, facing foreclosure. It ensures your long-term equity-building plan stays on track even during short-term disruptions.

Open Communication with Your Lender

If you see financial hardship on the horizon, do not stick your head in the sand. Proactively contact your lender. Most banks have hardship provisions and can offer temporary solutions, such as switching to interest-only payments for a short period (e.g., 12 months) or extending the loan term to lower monthly payments. While these measures don't actively build equity, they provide crucial breathing room to regain your financial footing and prevent a catastrophic outcome, allowing you to return to your equity-building plan later.

The journey from a 90 LVR loan to the security of 80% and below is a marathon, not a sprint. It demands discipline, financial literacy, and a proactive mindset. In today's interconnected and uncertain world, treating your mortgage as a dynamic entity to be actively managed is the hallmark of a savvy homeowner. By implementing a combination of aggressive repayments, strategic refinancing, value-adding improvements, and prudent safety nets, you can systematically dismantle the risks of high leverage and build a solid, resilient financial foundation for your future.
